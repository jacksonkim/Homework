Glossary

In a lot of these maintenance articles I will mention certain things by their beading term. Like crimp beads and jump rings. I will do what I can to provide definitions and/or pictures of the items I will be describing.

Crimp bead
a small malleable bead that, when pressed tightly between the ends of a plier, secure the end of a strand.

Jumpring
A small ring that connects jewelry compnents together. I typically use them to connect the beading wire to the clasp components.

Flat-head Plier
These are the pliers I use to close the crimp beads with. I smoosh the bead into a flat shape to secure the strand at the end. They look like normal pliers, without the ribbing on the inside.

Round-nose Pliers
These are the ones I use in conjuction with the flat-head plier. Because the nose is round on these, it makes it easier to curl the end of a strand in, or make a round end to connect components together.
